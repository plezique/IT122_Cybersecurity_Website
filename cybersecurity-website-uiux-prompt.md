# UI/UX Design Prompt: CyberSafe Learn

## Design Direction Reference
Inspired by the "Alethia" website aesthetic: dark, elegant, nature-meets-technology visual language, floating 3D elements, minimal glowing data overlays, and confident whitespace. Translate this mood into a **cybersecurity education** context — swap organic/environmental motifs for digital/security motifs while keeping the same premium, editorial feel.

---

## 1. Overall Aesthetic

- **Mood:** Elegant, quietly futuristic, trustworthy, premium — not flashy or "hacker-cliché"
- **Feel:** Like a high-end cybersecurity firm's site, not a student project — polished, confident, editorial
- **No emojis anywhere** on the site — use custom icons, line art, or data-inspired graphics instead
- Avoid clutter — generous negative space, let elements breathe like in the reference image

---

## 2. Color Palette

- **Primary background:** Deep near-black green or near-black navy (e.g., `#0A0F0C` or `#0B1210`) — dark, immersive, premium
- **Accent color:** A single glowing accent — electric green, cyan, or lime (e.g., `#C6FF3D` or `#39FF88`) used sparingly for highlights, CTAs, glowing data lines, and hover states
- **Secondary accent (optional):** A soft teal or blue for contrast in charts/data visuals
- **Text:** Off-white / soft white (`#F5F5F0`) for headings, muted gray (`#A8B0AA`) for body copy
- **Rule of thumb:** 90% dark neutral, 8% white/gray text, 2% glowing accent — accent should feel rare and intentional, like a signal in the dark

---

## 3. Typography

- **Style:** Simple, clean, highly readable — avoid decorative or overly geometric display fonts
- **Headings:** A modern grotesque/sans-serif with slightly condensed or monospaced character options (e.g., Space Grotesk, Inter, IBM Plex Sans, or Neue Montreal) — should feel technical but still elegant
- **Body text:** Same family or a clean pairing (e.g., Inter or IBM Plex Sans) for consistency and readability
- **Optional accent font:** A monospace font (e.g., IBM Plex Mono, JetBrains Mono) used sparingly for data labels, code snippets, terminal-style UI elements, or small tags — reinforces the "tech/security" feel without shouting it
- **Hierarchy:** Large, confident headlines (bold, tight line-height) contrasted with small, understated body text and labels
- **Letter-spacing:** Slightly increased tracking on nav items, labels, and small caps text (like "SOLUTIONS", "LET'S TALK" in the reference) for that refined, structured look

---

## 4. Imagery & Visual Motifs

Replace organic/rock-and-moss visuals with **cybersecurity/IT-themed equivalents** that carry the same "floating 3D object with glowing data overlay" concept:

- **Hero visual ideas:**
  - Abstract 3D shield, lock, or fragmented cube/node structure floating in dark space
  - Circuit-like or network-node geometry with glowing accent-colored connection lines
  - A stylized "data shard" or floating server/chip rendered with a similar organic-but-technical texture (moss → circuit traces, rock → matte metallic/ceramic surface)
- **Floating micro-elements:** Smaller versions of the same 3D object scattered around the page edges (mirroring the small rocks in the reference) — could be tiny padlocks, chip fragments, or geometric shards
- **Data overlay labels:** Small glowing text tags near visuals (e.g., `THREAT DETECTED`, `ENCRYPTED`, `VERIFIED`, status codes) styled in monospace, similar to the "-8.3 tCO2e / VERIFIED" tag in the reference — reinforces the "real-time intelligence" feel
- **Line art / wireframe accents:** Thin glowing outline paths tracing across visuals (like the yellow squiggle line in the reference) to suggest data flow, scanning, or network paths
- **Iconography:** Custom line-style icons (not filled, not emoji-style) for module categories — shield, lock, mail/phishing, network node, mobile device, eye/privacy — consistent stroke weight throughout

---

## 5. Layout & Structure

- **Navigation:** Fixed top nav, minimal — logo left, nav links center/right, primary CTA button top-right (rounded pill or slightly rounded rectangle, outlined or filled with accent color)
- **Hero section:** Large bold headline (2-3 lines max) top-left, short supporting paragraph bottom-left, floating 3D visual dominating the right/center of the viewport
- **Scroll cue:** Small "Scroll to discover" label bottom-right with subtle icon — reinforces the immersive, guided-journey feel
- **Section rhythm:** Full-width dark sections alternating subtle shade variations (not stark white breaks) to maintain immersion throughout the scroll
- **Cards/modules:** Minimal bordered cards with generous padding, subtle hover glow (accent-colored border or shadow on hover), icon + short title + one-line description
- **CTAs:** Consistent button style throughout — outlined rectangle/pill with arrow icon, accent color on hover or fill

---

## 6. Interactive & Micro-Interaction Details

- Subtle hover states: accent-color glow or underline animation on nav links and buttons
- Smooth scroll-triggered fade/slide-in animations for sections (not overly bouncy — should feel calm and controlled)
- Floating 3D elements can have slow, subtle parallax movement on scroll or mouse move (mirroring the floating rocks in the reference)
- Quiz/interactive tool components should maintain the same dark, glowing-accent style — e.g., correct answers highlighted in accent green, incorrect in a muted red/orange that still fits the palette
- Loading states or transitions can use a brief "scanning line" animation (thin glowing line sweeping across, like a security scan) for thematic reinforcement

---

## 7. Accessibility Notes

- Ensure sufficient contrast between accent color and dark background for text/CTAs (test against WCAG AA at minimum)
- Don't rely on glow effects alone to convey interactivity — pair with clear state changes (underline, border, icon change)
- Keep monospace/small-caps label text large enough to remain legible (avoid going below 12px)

---

## 8. Summary Direction Statement

> "A cybersecurity education platform that feels like the digital equivalent of a high-end research lab — dark, focused, quietly confident. Every glowing accent and floating element should feel like real-time intelligence at work, not decoration. Clean, elegant typography keeps it readable and approachable, while subtle tech-inspired visuals (network nodes, scan lines, data tags) keep the cybersecurity identity present in every section — never through clutter, always through restraint."
